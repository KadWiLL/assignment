The Twelve Pillars

Chapter 4 describes a dinner meeting between Charlie, an older maintenance man who worked at Twelve Pillars, and Michael, a mid-age man with a wife and two kids. Charlie worked for Mr. Davis, a very successful, wealthy man, and he is passing down some of his teachings about success to Michael, who feels he is not living up to his potential at work and has been having troubles in his relationship with his wife. A previous lesson from Charlie on how to put effort, time and imagination into his relationships, proved to be helpful for Michael. The advice to treat his relationhip like a garden inspired him to make time for a date night with his wife each week where she chooses whatever she wants to do.

In chapter 4, Charlie teaches him about the power of writing down goals, and tells him to start by writing down a hundred things he wants to accomplish before he dies. This process makes the mind aware of them and gets to work on how to achieve them. He then goes on to teach him on how to set long-range goals, mid-range goals, short-range goals and even micro-goals,emphasizing that the major reason for setting a goal is for it makes of one to accomplish that goal.

In chapter 5, Charlie continues to teach Michael about the proper use of time as he complained he wouldn't have the time to change the direction of his life when he is overwhelmed by work, wife and kids, etc. Here, Charlie talks about two types of pain: the pain of discipline and the pain of regret. When you are on the bed of death, the things you wanted to do in your lifetime, but you haven't been able to achieve will cause deep regrets. Charlie also mentions a time management trap that a lot of people fall into, that is spending their time on the urgentrather than the important. He also encourages Michael to be the designer of his own life, and to be the master of time rather than becoming a slave to it.

In chapter 6, Michael comes to share his achievements with Charlie at Twelve Pillars and he finds him cleaning the pool. His relationship with his wife kept improving and he wrote down the 100 goals of his life. When he asked about the next Pillar of Success, Charlie started to teach him about how to surround himself with the best people, about the power that people in his life have to influence and affect him. Together they start analyzing 3 of Michael's best friends, and he starts to see things in a different perspective led by Charlie's questions on what kind of influence they have on him, their attitudes and how they affect him. Charlie also talks about the importance of developing new relationships with successful people who will push him, challenge him and encourage him to become better. Hanging around and associating with more successful people will lead to three things: -he will open up to new knowledge about how the world works, how business works, how to better relationships; -he will open up to a vast new network; -his attitude will change. And that is important because 99% of life is attitude. And attittude is greatly shaped by influence and association.

In chapter 7, Charlie teaches Michael about the importance of learning, self-education, and reading books. Although he is resistant to the idea of reading books at first, Charlie is able to persuade him, when he refuses to meet Michael again until he has read one book. Charlie gives him a sheet of paper with Mr. Davis's list of "Books Everyone Should Read" and also offers him one of the books ("The Magic of Thinking Big") for free. Michael leaves the place feeling hopeful and reassured that he can continue to learn the Pillars of Success once he finishes reading his book.

In each chapter there is a lesson to be learnt, and a process of self-analyzing is triggered through the lenses of Michael's efforts to understand these pillars of success and how they apply to him and his situation. Personally, I believe I could benefit most from the pillar of success in chapter 4. Writing down my goals would help me to focus more my energy in that direction, acknowledge my goals and define them. Also, I believe learning to be the master of your time is crucial.
